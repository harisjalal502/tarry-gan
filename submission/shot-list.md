# Tarry Video Shot List

## Must-Have Real Shots

1. Reachy Mini sitting in the real office, visible on a desk.
2. Two teammates having a real debate near Reachy.
3. One teammate pointing at a whiteboard, paper sketch, or laptop screen.
4. Reachy visibly turning or reacting.
5. Laptop/dashboard shot showing camera view and bounding boxes.
6. Dashboard shot showing transcript/context cards/memory ledger.
7. GBrain retrieval answer shot.
8. Final hero shot of Reachy and the team in the room.

## Nice-To-Have Shots

1. Close-up of Reachy's head turning toward a speaker.
2. Over-the-shoulder shot of the dashboard.
3. Hands writing on the whiteboard.
4. Teammate saying the retrieval question out loud.
5. Quick cut of Slack/docs/calendar as the "digital context" contrast.
6. Outside/office/SF establishing shot.

## What To Avoid

1. Do not make the whole video look AI-generated.
2. Do not spend more than 3 seconds on Earth/SF zooms.
3. Do not show fake founder names as the product abstraction.
4. Do not over-explain GBrain. Show that memory search works.
5. Do not make the robot just a prop. It must visibly see or move.

## Real Office Blocking

Place Reachy near the center of the table. Put the dashboard laptop slightly behind or beside it so shots can include both robot and brain view. Put the whiteboard or sketch surface in the same direction the robot can plausibly look.

The best single shot is: Reachy in foreground, two teammates debating in background, dashboard visible with face boxes.
