# Product Brief

## One-Liner

Tarry turns the physical office into searchable team memory.

## Problem

Modern context systems are mostly digital. They capture calendar events, Slack threads, documents, tickets, commits, and CRM entries. But small teams make many important decisions in the physical world: at a whiteboard, in a debate, while preparing for a customer call, or in an unplanned office conversation.

That context is high-value and often unrecorded. The team later asks, "What did we decide?", "Why did we change the pitch?", or "What objection did we hear before the investor meeting?", and the answer is trapped in memory, not software.

## User

The first user is a small, intense startup team working in the same room. They do not want a heavy enterprise meeting-recorder workflow. They want an office companion that quietly captures useful context and can answer later.

## Product Promise

Tarry watches, listens, reacts, summarizes, and retrieves the physical context of a startup office.

## Why A Robot

The robot creates an obvious physical affordance. It can look at people, turn toward a whiteboard, respond out loud, and make the capture layer visible. A laptop webcam feels like surveillance. A small expressive office companion feels like a participant.

## Physical Layers

Tarry brings three non-digital context layers into the team memory system:

- Vision: camera frames, faces, room layout, whiteboards, screens, objects, and spatial attention.
- Audio: speech, exact phrasing, tone, interruptions, and conversational flow.
- Embodiment: gaze, head motion, reactions, and lightweight feedback that helps the room understand what the agent is attending to.

The third layer matters because the robot does not need to fully talk back to be useful. It can react, look toward the whiteboard, turn toward the current speaker, or signal that something sounded important.

## Core Use Cases

- Debate capture: track arguments, decisions, unresolved risks, and ownership.
- Whiteboard memory: observe sketches, keywords, pricing tables, ICP notes, or demo flow.
- Meeting prep: retrieve relevant context before an investor, customer, or internal strategy meeting.
- Team continuity: help someone who missed an in-room discussion catch up quickly.
- Ambient office recall: answer "What did we decide yesterday about X?"

## Context Cards

The dashboard should make the extracted context explicit. Cards should not just say "AI summary"; they should show the kinds of physical context being sent into memory:

- Decision: what the team agreed to.
- Risk: unresolved concern or objection.
- Question: something the team needs to answer.
- Follow-up: action item with owner when possible.
- Whiteboard: visual text, diagram, or room artifact.
- Attention: who or what the robot is looking at.
- Signal: a notable moment such as disagreement, excitement, or insight.

## What This Is Not Yet

This is not a full company knowledge base replacement. It is the missing physical-world input layer that can feed memory/search systems like GBrain.
